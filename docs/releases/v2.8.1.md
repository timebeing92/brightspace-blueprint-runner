# Blueprint Wizard v2.8.1

Blueprint Wizard v2.8.1 pairs the local terminal workflow with Blueprint
Bundle v1.3.2 and consumes its machine-readable delivery verdict.

## What changed

`coursecraft.progress/1` now gives the Wizard three distinct producer facts:

- `status` says whether the pipeline completed cleanly, partially, or with an
  error;
- `delivery.usable` says whether the emitted primary documents faithfully
  mirror the export;
- `delivery.empty` says the extracted course has no weekly structure and does
  not by itself make a run unusable.

The Wizard therefore:

- continues to present usable partial results with component findings;
- presents `delivery.usable: false` as a failed reading and does not offer the
  emitted fallback documents as a blueprint;
- labels a producer-approved empty course explicitly while keeping its result
  available;
- preserves the legacy behavior for Bundle producers through v1.3.0, which do
  not emit `delivery`;
- treats malformed falsy usability values conservatively and ignores unknown
  additive fields.

The Bundle still owns extraction, delivery classification, and the event
contract. The Wizard consumes those facts and does not infer fidelity by
globbing output files.

## Verification boundary

The automated suite covers normal success, usable partial, producer-declared
unusable partial, genuinely empty course, legacy events without `delivery`,
malformed known fields, and unknown additive fields. Release evidence and exact
Runner archive checksums are recorded alongside the published release rather
than predicted in this note.

The release remains unsigned and is not notarized. Managed installations
verify release metadata and SHA-256 checksums before activating a complete
Runner/Bundle pair; the previous installed version remains available for
rollback.
